About

Teaching that you can touch

I'm a computer science educator who believes the best way to learn how a computer works is to build it up one idea at a time — a switch, a gate, an adder, a processor — until the whole machine makes sense.

My teaching leans on active learning: interactive simulations, step-by-step visual traces, and questions that make students predict before they see the answer. I care about turning abstract theory into something students can drive with their own hands.

I'm especially excited about bringing AI & machine learning, cybersecurity, and the Internet of Things into the classroom in ways that are rigorous but genuinely fun.
